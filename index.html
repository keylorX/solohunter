About Me

I'm a cybersecurity researcher specializing in mobile security, vulnerability research, and Android application security. My work combines academic rigor with practical security analysis to advance the field of mobile threat detection and mitigation.

My research focuses on understanding attack surfaces in modern applications, discovering novel exploitation techniques, and developing defensive methodologies. I'm committed to publishing findings and contributing to the security community through responsible disclosure and academic collaboration.

Currently researching Android internals, deep link exploitation vectors, intent-based attacks, and automated detection frameworks for emerging threats.

With expertise in HackerOne bug bounty programs and active Android penetration testing, I bridge the gap between practical security work and academic research.
